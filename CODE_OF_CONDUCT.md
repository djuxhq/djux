# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Djux to be a practical, respectful project for Django developers. We expect contributors and maintainers to communicate clearly, assume good intent, and focus on improving the work.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Keep feedback specific and actionable.
- Welcome newcomers and reasonable questions.
- Credit ideas and contributions.
- Keep technical disagreement focused on the code, docs, and design.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks.
- Dismissive or hostile communication.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Spam or unrelated promotion.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, or block participation when behavior harms the project or contributors.

If you need to report a concern, open a private security/contact channel with the maintainers when available, or contact the repository owner directly.
